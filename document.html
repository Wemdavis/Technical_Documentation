INTRODUCTION

1.1 About the Employee Handbook

Numinous Fashion Brand Employee handbook (the "Handbook") has been established to provide rules and guidelines about the Firm's policies and practices.

  • It delineates, summarizes and explains all the policies, rules and regulations for every staff of the Firm and is not intended to act as a guarantee for employment for a fixed period of time.
  • This Handbook is supplemental to all other contract document, materials and information of the Firm. This document should not be considered as an all-encompassing material on all the policies relating to the Firm as the Firm may from time to time, disseminate information and other communications intended to alter or modify the provisions of this Handbook.
  • The policies and guidelines described herein are to be treated as important. Adherence to the provisions of this Handbook is a key requirement and condition of employment. Failure to comply with any provision of this Handbook may attract disciplinary actions.
  • This document replaces and supersedes any other previous employee handbook, rules, policies, manuals, guidelines or other policies of the Firm whether oral or in written communication.

1.2 Policy Modification

The Firm may, at anytime and at its sole discretion, change the terms of this Handbook. The terms of this Handbook can be revoked, revised, modified or altered in any manner the Firm sees fit.

  • All the employees of the Firm will be duly notified in writing if any change is made to this Handbook.
  • Every employee has the duty to read and understand the provisions of any modified version of this Handbook and adhere to its terms.
  • If there are questions or issues arising from any modified version, such issues should be duly communicated to supervisors, heads of department or the requisite human resources department in the Firm.

1.3 Voluntary At-Will Employment

Except as stated otherwise, all employment contract with the Firm is "at-will". This means that the Firm can, with or without cause, terminate the employment of any employee and any employee can, with or without cause, resign from their employment provided that adequate notice in writing has been furnished in accordance with the provisions of this Handbook.

Any promise, covenant or representations contrary to the tenets of this Handbook made by any officer of this Firm shall not be binding except such promises, covenants or representation is made in writing and duly authorized.

1.4 Dispute Resolution

You agree that any dispute or any issue whatsoever between you and the Firm will first, be reported to the Firm for resolution before having recourse to any other alternative dispute resolution mechanism.

If the matters remain unresolved, you agree that such unresolved matters will be referred to arbitration:

  • The parties shall select the arbitrators and the arbitration shall be conducted in the State where the Firm is located.
  • The arbitrator(s) shall be bound by applicable Federal and State laws of the Firm's place of business.

EMPLOYMENT POLICIES

3.1 Classification of Employment

Employees of the Firm will be classified in the manner described below:

  • Regular/Full-Time Employees: Regular/full-time employees are persons regularly scheduled to work for the following number of hours per week: 45.
  • Temporary Employees: Temporary employees are those employees who do not have a regular employment with the Firm, but have been employed to work for a limited period of: three months.
  1. Temporary employees may have their duration of employment extended provided that such extension is made in writing and signed by the Firm's management. The Firm may terminate the employment of a temporary worker with or without notice for any or no reason at all.
  2. All temporary employments are at-will employments.

For the purpose of employee classification, independent contractors, service providers, external solicitors,auditors and/or accountants and consultants are not considered as employees. Therefore, they are not entitled to leave benefits, retirement plan, insurance benefits and other employment benefits.

3.2 Probationary Period

All new employees will be on probation and on the job evaluation for the following period: One month. During this period, you are expected to:

  • consider your retention at the Firm.
  • After the period of probation, there will be performance appraisal for every new employee and subject to satisfactory report on your performance, your appointment will be confirmed.
  • The Firm's management may, in its absolute discretion, before the end of the probationary period, terminate the Employee's employment, for any reason and for no reason, with or without notice.

3.3 Outside Employment

Every employee is required to give their whole services to the Firm and are expressly prohibited from engaging in any other paid employment.

3.4 Non-Disclosure of Confidential Information

In the course of the employment with the Firm, employees may have access to non-public confidential and trade secret information regarding the business operation of the Firm.

Confidential Information includes all information or materials of whatever nature relating to the business operations of the Firm, any written material or by any oral or written statement whatsoever which includes but not limited to documents, techniques, practices, tools, specifications, inventions, patents, trademark, soft wares, drawings, programmes developed by or relating to the Firm or other information that is not known or easily ascertainable to the general public.

Confidential information does not include the following information or data:

  • which can be established by written records to be already known to the public at the time of the disclosure;
  • which enters the public domain through no fault of an employee;
  • is given by the Firm to third parties without any restrictions;
  • is given to you by any third party who is in possession of such information and has the legal right to disclose it; or
  • that is required by law to be disclosed.

As a condition of employment, you hereby agree as follows:

  1. To take proper and reasonable measures to ensure the confidentiality of the proprietary information;
  2. Not to make public, publish or otherwise disclose in whole or part, any information relating to the practice, business dealings or other matters relating to the Firm without obtaining the requisite consent;
  3. Not to use the confidential information for any purpose other than for the performance of designated tasks and assignments;
  4. To use all confidential Information and trade secret information to perform your designated duties and in accordance with your employment contract;
  5. Not to modify or alter any confidential or trade secret Information without the prior consent of your supervisor or the Firm's management as the case may be.
  6. You agree to comply with the provisions of this clause during and after the termination of your employment for as long as it remains a trade secret.

3.5 Conflict of Interest

Employees are required to, at all cost, avoid any relationship, association, business or situation that may conflict with the Firm's interests. Every employee should disclose all cases of potential conflicts of interest.

Conflicts of interests includes cases and situations where an employee forms, engages or participates in any business venture that is in direct competition with the Firm's business.

3.6 Non-Compete and Non-Solicitation Obligations

As a condition for your employment, you agree not to directly or indirectly engage in any business which is in competition with the Firm's business.

Directly or indirectly engaging in a competitive business includes but not limited to the following:

  • seeking to procure order from or do business with any client or customer of the Firm;
  • setting up a company or any other organization that engages in the provision of products or services offered by the Firm;
  • giving advice or working directly or indirectly for any person, business or any other organization that is in competition with the Firm as employee, independent contractor, director, consultant, etc during your employment with the Firm.

3.7 Privacy

The Firm takes the security of every employee's personal data very seriously and also takes reasonable measures to reduce the risk of unauthorized access to such information. Therefore, employee personal information will not be disclosed except as required for business operations and/or with the express consent of the employee.

Unauthorized copying, transmission or access to any employee's personal data by any employee shall be subject to discipline. Therefore,employees are encouraged to report any suspected data breach to the management.

3.8 Intellectual Property Ownership

As a condition of employment, you agree that the Firm retains the sole ownership of any intellectual property and related materials which includes but not limited to trade marks, trade secrets, goodwill, patents, copyrights etc. which you developed or produced during the performance of your tasks and duties for the Firm and the Firm shall be granted unrestricted and unrestrained access to use such intellectual property.

3.9 Transfer of Employment

In the event that the Firm is being acquired by another organization, an employee may be transferred to the acquiring organization with the express consent of the employee upon such terms and conditions as may be agreed by the parties.

  • If an employee remains absent from work and fails to notify their supervisor about such absence within a consecutive period of: 3 working days, such continued absence will be considered as resignation from work.

LEAVES AND HOLIDAYS

6.1 General Provision

Regular attendance is mandatory for every employee.

However, employees are entitled to certain holidays and leave. In addition to holidays required by law, the Firm's management may from time to time approve leave requests depending on an employee's circumstance.

Every leave must be duly authorized and approved by the Firm's management prior to the period the leave is taken. Except for sick or medical leaves, a leave may be approved, denied or postponed to a later date. An extension of leave may be approved before the expiration of the current leave.

After a leave request has been approved, every employee is required to properly handover all pending tasks to their supervisor and/or the Firm's management as the case may be.

Except as required by law, there is no guarantee that an employee will be reinstated in the exact position or titled they held before the leave. However, the Firm will take all reasonable steps to ensure that all employees retain their former position they held prior to the leave.

6.2 Annual Leave

In addition to public holidays, every regular employee is eligible for annual leave for a period of: ________ per calendar year.

The annual leave must be taken at the times convenient to the Firm and sufficient notice of intention to take leave must be given to the employee's supervisor and the Firm's management prior to the period the leave will be taken.

All annual leaves must be approved by the Firm's management before an employee can take the annual leave.

6.3 Parental Leave

According to the law, every female employee shall due to pregnancy and upon presentation of a medical certificate, be entitled to maternity leave for a minimum period of six weeks before delivery and another period of six weeks after delivery.

  • However, the leave may be extended upon the prior approval of the management of the Firm's management prior to the period the leave will be taken.
  • At the conclusion of maternity leave, the employee will be restored to their previous position or a similar position in the Firm. An employee on maternity leave shall be entitled to 50% of the Employee's salary for the duration of the leave.
  • All maternity leaves must be approved by the Firm.

6.4 Sick Leave

Every Employee is entitled to a paid sick leave for a period of 3 (three) days in one calendar year for any temporal illness certified by a certified medical practitioner. However, depending on the nature of the illness, the Firm's management prior to the period the leave will be taken, may approve a leave extension. All medical leaves must be approved by the Firm's management.

At the conclusion of the sick leave and prior to an employee's return to work, the employee is required to obtain a certificate of fitness from their doctor to be presented to the Firm's management.

6.5 Compassionate Leave

A compassionate leave may be granted to an employee for personal circumstances. Personal circumstances include the following:

  • Close family member's illness: the care of an employee's spouse, child, partner suffering from a severe illness.
  • Close family member's injury: the care of an employee's spouse, partner or child who has been severely injured.
  • Family emergency: to attend to family emergencies ranging from child's welfare, death of a close family member, etc.

The compassionate leave shall be taken for a period of: three days, per calendar year.

When a leave is requested to care for a family member who is severely sick or injured, the employee shall be required to present a medical certificate signed by the relative's medical practitioner and containing the following information:

  1. the nature of the condition and the date the condition started;
  2. an estimated duration or period of the condition;
  3. an estimated amount of time that the medical practitioner believes that the employee will be required to provide the necessary care and support to the sick or injured family member;
  4. a statement indicating that the care and support of the employee is required.

6.6 Study Leave

An Employee may be entitled to study leave for a period of 90 (ninety) days and such leave must be granted at the request of the employee and consent of the Firm's management must be obtained.

6.7 Meetings and Conferences

Employees may be given some time off to participate in educational opportunities related to the employee's current or anticipated work with the Firm.

However, the Firm does not grant leave or time-off to employees to attend meetings and conferences that are regarded as beneficial to the and/or enhance their professional growth and development.

ELECTRONIC DEVICES AND INTERNET POLICIES

7.1 Computers and Handheld Devices

The Firm may provide computers for employees to ensure the effective execution and performance of tasks and duties.

Employees must ensure that the use of computers conforms with the primary purpose for which it was provided. The Firm prohibits the use of the Firm's electronic devices for personal use.

The Firm retains ownership of all computers provided by the Firm and the Firm's management retains the right to inspect and monitor data or files at any given time.

Personal devices can create distractions in a work place, hence, the use of personal phones and mobile devices should be limited during working hours. In particular, the use of personal devices should be done in accordance with the following policy:

  • No employee is allowed to use their personal phones during working hours except during break periods and cases of emergency.
  • If an employee's personal device is found to be disruptive and reduce the employee's level of productivity, such employee may face disciplinary actions.

7.2 Information Security

The Firm maintains a strict policy on confidential electronic information which every employee (who have access to such information) must strictly adhere to.

  • Employees should ensure that no printed document relating to the Firm, the Firm's clients, customers, or visitors is left unattended on their work desks.
  • Employees who work with computers or mobile devices provided by the Firm should ensure that they use a secure password on all those devices and ensure that they promptly notify management if the device is lost or damaged.
  • The Firm expressly prohibits any unauthorized use or distribution of confidential information or documents. All confidential information or documents that must be transferred within the Firm should be done through the following means:
  1. Not acceptable except in the cases where it is approved by authorities.
  2. If any staff of this Firm discovers that a computer system does not conform to this Policy, such an employee is required to promptly notify management, so that appropriate actions can be taken.

7.3 Internet Acceptable Use

This internet policy applies to every employee who has access to the Firm's computers and mobile devices.

The Firm's internet must not be used to access, transmit, print, download any derogatory, obscene, defamatory or offensive material, such as anything that is used to cause disparagement or harassment.

Unauthorized download or dissemination of copyrighted material using the Firm's internet is prohibited. Permission to download copyrighted material must be sought and obtained from the owner or originator of the material.

Employees are expressly prohibited from downloading, copying installing video games, videos, pictures or other Unauthorized Materials with the Firm's internet.

Unauthorized Materials are documents, files, videos, pictures or any other material that are not related or relevant to the achievement or performance of an employee's designated task or duties.

The use of the Firm's internet for personal work is expressly prohibited.

7.4 Email and Computer Use

The Firm allows various means of communication and dissemination of information. This may include: e-mail, text message, instant messaging, voice messages and voice calls, which may be used via mobile devices or computers.

Employees may not use E-mails for disseminating or transmitting information or materials that are offensive, disparaging, copyrighted materials or unauthorized sharing of confidential information or trade secrets. Employees may not encrypt programmes or install any encrypted software or programme with any email communication.

Employees who use the Firm's computers should handle Firm's computers with care as they may be held responsible for their loss or damage.

Every employee of the Firm is required to refrain from the following:

  • the unauthorized download of software on Firm's computers;
  • the use of devices such as computers and handheld devices for disseminating or transmitting confidential information or trade secrets of the Firm;
  • using Firm-owned computers or devices to disseminate, transmit, or download disparaging, obscene, or illicit materials;
  • downloading, copying, installing video games, videos, pictures or other Unauthorized Materials on the Firm's computers.

7.5 Social Media Policy

The Firm is determined to use social media to create awareness about its services, enhance its customer/client base and increase the overall profitability of the Firm. Employees are strongly encouraged to use social media platforms to promote the activities of the Firm.

Employees should maintain the confidentiality of clients/customers, other employees and the Firm by ensuring that personal information are not disclosed on social media. Employees should also note that the use of abusive, disparaging words, any form of threat or harassment on social media is highly prohibited.

Firm-related social media activities will be monitored and any employee who is found guilty of violating this policy will face disciplinary actions.

8.3 Discipline for Non-Compliance

This Handbook provides the list of prohibited behaviour or conduct. While it is impossible for the Firm to outline the code of conduct for every situation, the Firm has provided general guidelines for employees' conduct.

The following list does not represent a comprehensive list of all misconduct as the Firm may, from to time and on a case by case basis, formulate rules regulating employee's code of conduct in the Firm ("Misconduct"):

  • theft or unauthorized possession of Firm's property;
  • theft or unauthorized possession of any property belonging to an employee of the Firm;
  • engaging in physical and/or verbal abuse in the workplace;
  • deceit and other fraudulent practices;
  • unsatisfactory work performance and lack of professionalism;
  • incessant refusal to adhere to the rules of the Firm;
  • illegal possession of fire arms and other harmful objects;
  • inappropriate or unauthorized use of the Firm's confidential information;
  • frequent lateness to work;
  • failure to perform required tasks and duties on time;
  • insubordination;
  • willful destruction of Firm's property;
  • failure to comply with the provisions of this Handbook.

If an employee is found guilty of Misconduct, the Firm's management shall at its sole discretion, take disciplinary actions against such employee. Disciplinary actions taken may include, but not limited to suspension, salary deductions, reassignment, or dismissal.

The Firm will impose the following disciplinary measures for the following categories of misconduct: For cases of verbal and physical abuse at the work place, if the offending party is found guilty, such a person will be penalized/dismissed. In addition to the disciplinary measures outlined above, the Firm may, from time to time impose guidelines for addressing issues of Misconduct.

8.4 Procedure for Handling Complaints

Employees who have suffered harassment, discrimination, verbal or physical abuse or any violation of any type, should report to their supervisor or the Firm's management as the case may.

Upon receipt, the management of the Firm shall conduct an inquiry aimed at ascertaining the veracity of such complaints and after due investigations have been conducted, the Firm may impose any disciplinary action against the offending party.

Upon receipt of any complaint by an employee, the Firm shall do as follow: Firstly, the company's management will set up a panel to investigate claims made by the employee. then the offending party will be brought before the panel to state their case.
